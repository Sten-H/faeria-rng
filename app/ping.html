FAQ

What is a ping?

A ping in card games usually refers to dealing 1 damage. In faeria there are some cards that will send n amount of pings randomly between all enemy creatures and the enemy god (representation of enemy player).

Can a target be damaged beyond its hp?

No once it has received a ping that brings its hp to 0 it is no longer considered a target for remaining pings. So if you have 4 pings and 2 creatures with 2 hp, they will both die in all outcomes.

Is this working properly?

Not right now, pings can hit creatures that have died. It still generates correct results if there is one creature set to live with hp > ping amounts. This is because then every overdamage ping can be counted as a ping on that creature, and we don't care about that creature's life status in that situation. This is of course not intended behaviour and should be fixed.

Why is performance so bad with high ping amounts and many creatures?

If creature amount is n and ping amount is k the complexity is O(nkn). nk are the number of outcomes. Each creature marked to die then filters the outcomes for only outcomes where it dies. If there are 5 creatures (2 set to die) and 9 pings that's 592 = 3 906 250 . Pretty bad, huh? Making complexity just nk should be pretty easy.
